\label{chap:related}
This chapter reviews the related work in the field of \gls{CRC} detection using \gls{DL} models. It covers the challenges faced by researchers and the various approaches taken to address these difficulties. Furthermore, this chapter highlights the gaps in the existing literature and outlines how this dissertation aims to fill those gaps.

\section{Dataset Usage}
\label{sec_dataset_usage}
Hossain \& Shaban \cite{DBLP:conf/aibthings/HossainS24} compared the results of residual networks and the Visual Geometry Group 16 architecture using the \gls{LC25000} \gls{DS}. Although the primary objective of their article was to find a resource efficient \gls{DL} architecture for \gls{CRC} detection, the results were extremely high, exceeding 99\% across all evaluated metrics \cite{metrics}. Given these exceptionally high scores, there is a valid concern regarding whether models trained exclusively on this \gls{DS} can generalize effectively to image sets from alternative sources, a problem that is explored in Chapter~\ref{chap:methodology}.

Kather et al. made available a dataset containing one hundred thousand histological images of human colorectal cancer and healthy tissue \cite{kather2018crc100k}. Researchers Tripathi et al.~\cite{colonML} utilized traditional \gls{ML} algorithms, such as \gls{KNN} and \gls{RFR}, to classify colon tissue, but their study lacks the inclusion of \gls{DL} models. In Chapters \ref{chap:methodology} and \ref{chap:results}, we address this gap by exploring \gls{DL} architectures trained on different available datasets and validated with images from external sources~\cite{borkowski2019lungcoloncancerhistopathological, kather_2016_53169}.

Mulyadi \& Utaminingrum~\cite{10913097} explored different configurations of residual networks combined with a self-attention layer for colon tissue classification using the \gls{CRC5000} dataset alongside ImageNet standard normalization \cite{DBLP:conf/nips/KrizhevskySH12}. However, their study did not include classic \gls{CNN} architectures for comparison.

\section{Quanvolution}
\label{sec_quanvolution_related_works}
Sobrinho et al.~\cite{quanvolution} explored the potential of \gls{QML} for breast cancer detection using the \gls{BC} dataset. The conclusion of their work demonstrated that quantum models can achieve competitive performance compared to more complex, traditional \gls{CNN} models. This outcome raises important questions: Can this quantum solution be successfully expanded to other medical use cases, such as \gls{CRC} detection? Furthermore, can it be effectively applied to colon tissue detection?
