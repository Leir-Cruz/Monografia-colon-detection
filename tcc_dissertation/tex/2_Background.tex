\section{Machine Learning and Computer Vision}
\label{sec_machine_learning}

In 1943 the first mathematical model of what would be the bases of \gls{AI}, field of computer science dedicated to creating systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, was published by Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts \cite{mcculloch1943}. The article evalueats the neuron activity as a character of "all or none", where their events can be treated by propositional logic. Biologically, the brain cell has needs to receive an excitation higher than it`s thredhold to trigger an impulse, which is determined by their own characteristcs.

Considering that, the authors claim that a neuron can be described as a logic block, executing logical function. Brain cells could be organized in nets that could describe any logical preposition. In 1950, with the question 'Can machines think?', Alan Turing starts his paper \cite{DBLP:journals/x/Turing50}, describing thinking habilities as problem-solving, decision-making, and pattern recognition. In this approach, intelligence could be simulated by computational process. However, there is philosophical question to be made that is what exactly is intelligence? By stating that machines could emulate human intelligence, it`s necessary to delimit what it is and how it can be mesaured. In his article, Turing affirms that years from there, machines would be able to similute human behavior in such a way, that a regular person would not be able to distinguish whether is a machine or not they are interacting with.


By comparing the human brain to digital computer, Turing suggests that it was not necessary to build a machine comparable to an adult human intelligence capacity, but one with capacity of apprenticeship. Suggesting that a "child machine" should be subject to "education" through rewards and punishments, analogous to how children learn. \gls{ML} is an area of knowledge that focus on developing algorithms that allow computer system to learn from data and improve their performance without being explicit programmed. This mathematical relationship derived from the data is called model. Each revelant feature, or characteristc of data, must be represented numerically, so the model can learn. In \cite{5392560}, Samuel, A. L shows a computer that learns to play a game called checker better than the person who wrote the program. The game was chosen because of the simplicity of its rules and transparency of the computer learning progress.

Not only in \gls{AI} context, data organization is more important than ever in the XXI century, considering that companies collect large amounts of data from various sources daily. Considering that, data must often be organized collections that make information accessible for analysis and application - called \gls{DS}.
However, there are more than one way of storing data, and different types of \gls{DS} store it in various ways. 

Structured \gls{DS} organize information in predefined structure, normally tables which each column is an attribute and each row an instance. In other hand, unstructured \gls{DS} contain information that does not fit in a rigid schema or cannot be resumed to a numerical or text value. Some examples of it can be images, videos, web-pages, documents or even applications. Futhermore, semistructured \gls{DS} don't follow rigid schemas, they incorporate defined syntax, such as \gls{JSON}, \gls{HTML} or \gls{XML}.

The type of task that can be solved or explored using \gls{ML} and \gls{AI} depends not only in the quality of data, but the type of the \gls{DS} that will train the model.

Not only that, but also was compromissed in understand how information contained in storage influence regonition. Such as Alan Turing \cite{DBLP:journals/x/Turing50}, Resenblatt also belived in the application of positive or negative reinforcement to make the system learn. Not only that, but also expanded to his machine model the biological concept that similar stimulus should activate the same nets cells.

This probabilistic model called perceptron is divided in three layers: Sensority units that respondes to impulses in all or nothing bases; Association units receives the transmitted impulses and, if the algebric sum of those are greated than the unit thredhold, it fires and generates the responses of some Response units. Each response unit has feedbacks connections that can be excitatory or inhibitory and are mutually exclusive. This means that, if a response unit is trigged during a stimulus, it will inhibit others from trigger to other similar ones.

\begin{equation}
    \epsilon(k) = d(k) - [a_0 + \sum_{j=1}^{n} a_j s_j(k)]

\section{Artificial Neural Networks and Gradient Descent}
\label{sec_neural_networks}
The perceptron \cite{rosenblatt1958perceptron} can be considered as the first published attempet to build a \gls{ANN} that could learn from examples. However, it was only a single layer aside from input and output. Later, in his book Principles of Neurodynamics \cite{Rosenblatt1963PRINCIPLESON}, the author explicit discusses multilayer perceptron architectures, with multiple neurons organized in series. As in figure \ref{fig:adjustable neuron}, the output of a neuron is connect serves as input of the next one.

It was with David E. Rumelhart et al \cite{Rumelhart1986LearningRB} that an \gls{ANN} training procedure was shown useful in practice, because of the high cost of adjusting all weights of the neuron. Those extra layers between output and input were called hidden-layers, or hiddens units, and they introduce more difficult to find the learning rules, or the corrects weights, to reduce the difference between the actual and desired outputs. The input of a neuron in j layer is a linear function of the outputs ($y_i$) multiplied by their respective weights ($w_{ij}$).
\begin{equation} 
    x_j = \sum_{i} y_i w_{ij} 
\end{equation}
One important difference pointed in \cite{Rumelhart1986LearningRB} is each hidden-layer output is followed by a non-linear function, different than \gls{ADALINE}, which all layers had as output a linear-function. This occurs because series linear function only could be simply replaced by a single one, just adjusting the weight, which would limit the system capacity of learning.
For example, in binary classification, as explored in chapter \ref{chap:methodology}, is very common to use sigmoid function as the non-linear function of the neurons output:
\begin{equation}
     y_j = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-x_j}} \label{sigmoid_func}
\end{equation}
David appointed that the total error of an multi-layered \gls{ANN} could be, similar to perceptron, as the comparison of the desired output and the prediction
\begin{equation} 
    E = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{c} \sum_{j} (y_{j,c} - d_{j,c})^2 
\end{equation}
where ($c$) is an index over cases, ($j$) is index over outputs units and ($y$) the actual state of the output. The main point, as in Rosenblatt article \cite{Rosenblatt1963PRINCIPLESON}, is minimize ($E$). To make this, is suggested the use of \gls{GD} \cite{5a25e804-97fa-3be4-ac27-50be05d4f28d}, mathematical method to find local points of minima.
By calculating the partial derivatives of the error and applying calculus chain rule, it is possible how the change in the input or the weight of a layer ($j$) affects the error:
\begin{equation} 
    \frac{\partial E}{\partial x_j} = \frac{\partial E}{\partial y_j} y_j (1 - y_j) 
\end{equation} 

\begin{equation} 
    \frac{\partial E}{\partial w_{ij}} = \frac{\partial E}{\partial x_j} y_i 
\end{equation}

The derivates above propagates from the top layer back to the bottom one after, updating the weights of the \gls{ANN}:

\begin{equation} 
    \Delta w(t) = -\epsilon \frac{\partial E}{\partial w(t)} + \alpha \Delta w(t-1) 
\end{equation}

Where ($t$) where ¢ is incremented by 1 for each sweep through the whole set of input-output cases, and ($\alpha$) is an exponential decay factor between 0 and 1 that determines the contribution of the current gradient and earlier gradients to the weight change.

 This new method of learning with \gls{BP} and \gls{GD} made multilayer neural networks available in every-day use, overcomming perceptron limitation that could only solve linearly separable problems.
