\gls{CRC} is a type of tumor that affects the large intestine and the rectum, responsible for processing food waste, absorbing water and nutrients. After evaluating the symptoms, the diagnosis is confirmed by exams such as occult blood test in stool, \gls{CL} and biopsy. For successful treatment, early diagnosis of the disease is essential. However, the absence of symptoms in the early stages, associated with lack of access to screening exams, contributes to late diagnosis.
The goal of this work is the use of deep neural networks for early detection of \gls{CRC} using biopsy images. The proposed methodology involves the comparison of different architectures, from the most classic ones like AlexNet, to the use of residuals like ResNet. Futhermore, it includes the early-adoption of quantum convolutional layers in \gls{CRC} classification.
The results obtained prove how the use of \gls{ML} can be useful for early detection of \gls{CRC}, contributing to more accurate diagnosis and treatments, increasing the chances of cure and improving the quality of life of patients.
