\section{Problem Statement}
\label{sec_problem}

Worldwide, colorectal cancer is one of the most common and deadly types of cancer. The third most comon cancer, accounting for aproximately 10\% of all cases, it is the second leading cause of cancer-related deaths. In 2022 alone, there were an estimated 1.9 million new cases and more than 900,000 deaths globally.
The symptons may include blood in the stool, changes in bowel habits,such ass diarreia or constipation, abdominal pain and unexplained weight loss. 

Besides being more common in individuals over 50 years old, there are a lot of factors that can increase the risk of developing the disease, such as low diet in fruits and vegetables, high consumption of red and processed meats, physical inactivity and obsesity. Alcohol consumption and smoking are also associated with an increased risk of developing colorectal cancer. In addition, there are some hereditary conditions that can increase the risk, such as familial adenomatous polyposis (FAP) and Lynch syndrome.
People with inflammatory bowel disease have higher chances of colon cancer, the worse the severity of the inflammation.

The most efficient way to treat, and even cure, a pacient diagnostic with colorectal cancer, is through early detection of the tumor. However, colonoscopy is a very invasive procedimment and not always can detect precisely the presence ou absence of the polyps, which are the main precursors of colorectal cancer. Futhermore, the acuracy of colonoscopy can be affected by third-party factors, such as the experience of the endoscopist and the quality of the bowel preparaiton. In addition, the lack of access to screening exams, especially in low-income regions, contributes to late diagnosis and worse prognosis for patients with colorectal cancer. Therefore, there is a need for more accurate and efficient methods for early detection of colorectal cancer, which can improve patient outcomes and reduce the burden of this disease on healthcare systems worldwide.



\section{Research Objectives}
\label{sec_goals}

To improve the early detection of colorectal cancer, this work aims to analyze diferents architectures of deep neural networks for the classification of colonoscopy images. In addition, the study also includes diferentes networks architectures, such as quantum neural networks, to evaluate their performance in a low deep scenario. The main goal is to develop a more accurate and efficient method for early detection of colorectal cancer, which can improve patient outcomes and reduce the burden of this disease on healthcare systems worldwide.
