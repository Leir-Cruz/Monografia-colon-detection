\section{Problem Statement}
\label{sec_problem}

Worldwide, colorectal cancer is one of the most common and deadly types of cancer. The third most common cancer, accounting for approximately 10\% of all cases, it is the second leading cause of cancer-related deaths. According to Our World Data ong  {owid-cancer}, there were an estimated 1.9 million new cases and more than 900,000 deaths globally in 2022 alone.
The symptoms include blood in the stool, changes in bowel habits, such as diarrhea or constipation, abdominal pain and unexplained weight loss. 

Besides being more common in individuals over 50 years old, studies \cite{DEKKER20191467} show that there are a lot of factors that can escalate the risk of developing the disease, such as low diet in fruits and vegetables, high consumption of red and processed meats, physical inactivity and obesity. Alcohol consumption and smoking are also associated with an increased risk of developing colorectal cancer. In addition, there are some hereditary conditions that can increase the risk, such as familial adenomatous polyposis (FAP) and Lynch syndrome.
People with inflammatory bowel disease have higher chances of colon cancer, the worse the severity of the inflammation.

The most efficient way to treat, and even cure, a pacient diagnosed with colorectal cancer, is through early detection of the tumor. However, colonoscopy is a very invasive procedure and not always can detect precisely the presence or absence of the polyps, which are the main precursors of colorectal cancer. Furthermore, the accuracy of colonoscopy can be affected by third-party factors, such as the experience of the endoscopist and the quality of the bowel preparation. In addition, the lack of access to screening exams, especially in low-income regions, contributes to late diagnosis and worse prognosis for patients with colorectal cancer. Therefore, there is a need for more accurate and efficient methods for early detection of colorectal cancer, which can improve patient outcomes and reduce the burden of this disease on healthcare systems worldwide.

\section{Research Objectives}
\label{sec_goals}

To improve the early detection of colorectal cancer, this work aims to analyze different architectures of deep neural networks for the classification of colonoscopy images. In addition, the study also includes different networks architectures, such as quantum neural networks, to evaluate their performance in a low deep scenario. The main goal is to develop a more exact and efficient method for early detection of colorectal cancer, which can improve patient outcomes and reduce the burden of this disease on healthcare systems worldwide.


\section{Organization of This Document}
\label{sec_about}


This thesis is organized in five chapters, including this introduction. Chapter 2 presents the scientific foundation of this work, details of colorectal cancer, what is machine learning and deep learning and the structure of the neural networks used in this paper. Also, the methrics used to evaluate the performance of the models are described in this chapter. Chapter 3 presents other related works that have been done in the area of computer vision applied to medical images, with a focus on the ones that use colonoscopy images. Chapter 4 illustrates the methodology used in this study, including the set of images used, their transformation and details of training. Finally, chapter 5 approaches the results obtained and their analysis, while chapter 6 concludes the work, with a summary of the main contributions and future works.
