\section{Discussion}

The 100\% accuracy in some cases of the train and test using \gls{LC25000} suggests overfitting. By analysing the \gls{DS} original paper \cite{borkowski2019lungcoloncancerhistopathological}, it`s described that the twenty five thousand images were the outcome of an augmentation process in the original seven hundred fifty images - five hundreds images from colon that were augmentated to ten thousand. Considering that, there`s a high probability of the same base image being used during train, validation and test, only variating from rotation or flips.

The Loss images from it`s training process in this section show that, specially with the scheduler, that the Loss curve was converging to the global minimal. However, the quality of the samples were questionable. Due to institution permission, the original data was not provided in the article, making it impossible to split train and test images correctly.

The results in tables \ref{table_lc25000_adam}, \ref{table_lc25000_sgd} and \ref{table_lc25000_adammax} show that, for another set of images, \gls{LC25000} did not have a good performance to validate others images \gls{DS} without any color normalization technique \cite{5193250}, even with validation and train loss converging to global minimal. However, AlexNet tensor had great performance, specially using adammax \cite{DBLP:journals/corr/KingmaB14} and sgd \cite{articleDocReg} optimizers, what suggests that, as the train set images were originally from the same five hundred images, a small number of samples, the more complex models such as Residual nets and the visual geometry group-16 had difficult in generalize the parameters.

When training with \gls{NCTCRCHE100K}, the results were very different and metrics variates between two scenarios: using or not the "no-norm" variantion of the \gls{DS} and the test with different sources sets of images. Tables \ref{table_NCTCRCHE100K_adam},\ref{table_NCTCRCHE100K_adammax} and \ref{table_NCTCRCHE100K_sgd} results advocate that the models trained with this image set performed great, with all evaluation metrics testing with image set from the same \gls{DS} being above above ninety-six percent . Moreover, recorded a downfall of ten percent when evaluating with \gls{CRC5000}, what could, at a first impression, be explained to the fact the \gls{CRC5000} original size images being of 150 x 150 pixel resolution, instead of the resized size of 224 x 224, causing loss of sharpness in the image sample.

However, comparing the results in tables \ref{table_NCTCRCHE100K-nonorm_adam},\ref{table_NCTCRCHE100K-nonorm_adammax} and \ref{table_NCTCRCHE100K-nonorm_sgd}, it`s possible to see a downfall in the performance using the test images from \gls{NCTCRCHE100K}, what can be explained due to the different normalizations in NCT-CRC-HE-100K-NONORM and CRC-VAL-HE-7K, as exposed in section \ref{subsec_nct100k}. The major difference is the result validating with \gls{CRC5000} and \gls{LC25000}. The impromivement in the first one comparing to the standard NCT-CRC-HE-100K reveals that the the increment in their respective image sizes did not have a significant impact in the models as it first estimation. This indicats that the main reason of the downfall in the previous scenario was the difference in color normalization techniques applied to the images set used for train and test \cite{5193250}.

This conclusion is also reinforced by the outcome of the \gls{LC25000}, illustrated by the difference between \ref{fig:100K_adam_lc2500}, \ref{fig:100K_adammax_lc2500} and \ref{fig:100K_sgd_lc2500} tables and \ref{fig:100K-nonorm_adam_lc2500}, \ref{fig:100K-nonorm_adammax_lc2500} and \ref{fig:100K-nonorm_sgd_lc2500}. In the first case, all models were always predicting all images from \gls{LC25000} as "non-cancer", most likely due to Macenko's method color normalization in training set. However, by removing this feature in the train stage, outcomes were more diverse and close to real classification. Performance was not great, which in that case could be explained original dimension of \gls{LC25000} samples, which were 768 x 768 pixels.
